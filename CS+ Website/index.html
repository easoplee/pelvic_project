3D Bone Modeling

About the Project

This project is affiliated with the Duke University Department of Computer Science and Department of Radiology. Through collaboration in the Mazurowski Lab, this website was developed to help visualize bones from MRI scans. The website takes in a .npy 3D MRI image and based on user input, converts the selected bones into a 3D, interactive model of them (similar to the model of the femur to the left). The model is created using bone segmentation algorithms that identify different bones in the scans. We hope this will make MRI imaging more accessible and help people better visualize the human body.

Some other research interests of Dr. Mazurowski include developing rigorous engineering solutions to real problems in medicine. The Mazurowski Lab works on a wide range of applications including cancer and musculoskeletal imaging. Some recent technical interests include domain adaptation and generalization, anomaly detection, and class imbalance in convolutional neural networks.
